Understanding the Language of Money

Master the core vocabulary to navigate the financial world with confidence.

Leverage

Meaning: Using borrowed resources or tools to increase potential returns.

Why it matters:

  • Can accelerate wealth building
  • Increases both opportunity and risk
  • Used in business and investing

Investing

Meaning: Putting money into assets that grow in value over time.

Types of investments:

  • Stocks & Bonds
  • Real estate
  • Mutual funds & Index funds
  • Businesses

Stocks

Meaning: Ownership shares in a company.

Key idea:

  • When you buy stock, you own part of a business
  • Value changes based on performance and market demand

Bonds & Treasury Bonds

Meaning: Loans given to governments or companies in exchange for interest payments.

Treasury bonds:

  • Issued by governments
  • Lower risk, steady returns
  • Long-term financial instruments

Market Trends

Meaning: The overall direction in which financial markets move.

Includes:

  • Bull markets (growth)
  • Bear markets (decline)
  • Cycles of expansion and correction

Controlling Your Money

You cannot build wealth without controlling your cash flow. Wealth grows from consistency, not randomness.

Budgeting Basics

  • Track: Monitor income and expenses
  • Separate: Needs vs Wants
  • Allocate: Savings first
  • Plan: Monthly spending limits

Financial Structure:
Income → Expenses → Savings → Investments

How to Start Investing in the NSE (Kenya)

A practical pathway to entering the Kenyan stock market.

CDS Account (Central Depository System)

To trade on the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE), you need a CDS account. It holds your shares electronically and allows buying and selling stocks.

  • 1. Open Account: Choose a licensed stockbroker, fill form, provide ID/passport photo and KRA PIN.
  • 2. Verify: Submit documents and receive CDS account number.
  • 3. Trade: Deposit funds, buy stocks via broker platform, and monitor portfolio.
