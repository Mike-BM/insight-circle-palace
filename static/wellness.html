What Your Body Needs

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Why it matters:
  • Supports brain function and memory
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Supports heart health
  • Improves mood stability
Sources: Salmon, sardines, mackerel, chia seeds, flaxseeds, walnuts.

Vitamin A

Why it matters:
  • Supports vision and skin health
  • Boosts immune system
Sources: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach.

Vitamin D

Why it matters:
  • Strengthens bones
  • Supports immunity
  • Improves mood regulation
Sources: Sunlight, eggs, fortified foods.

Vitamin C

Why it matters:
  • Supports skin repair
  • Boosts immunity
  • Helps iron absorption
Sources: Oranges, lemons, berries, peppers.

Vitamin B Complex

Why it matters:
  • Energy production
  • Brain and nerve health
Sources: Eggs, whole grains, legumes, meat.

Calcium & Magnesium

Why it matters:
  • Bone strength
  • Muscle relaxation
  • Nervous system support
Sources: Milk, leafy greens, nuts, seeds.

Water & Hydration

Hydration affects energy levels, digestion, skin clarity, and brain focus.

  • Drink water consistently throughout the day
  • Start mornings with water
  • Reduce excessive sugary drinks
  • Increase intake during heat or activity

Gut Health & Digestion

Constipation Causes: Low fiber, dehydration, lack of movement, irregular eating.

Prevention & Relief:

  • Eat fiber-rich foods (fruits, vegetables, oats)
  • Drink enough water daily
  • Include probiotics (yogurt, fermented foods)
  • Move your body regularly (walking, exercise)
  • Maintain consistent meal times

Daily Movement Matters

"You don’t need extreme workouts — you need consistent movement."

  • Light daily exercise (walking, stretching)
  • Strength training basics
  • Bodyweight exercises
  • Importance of consistency over intensity

Sleep & Body Restoration

Sleep affects hormones, memory, mood, skin health, and your immune system.

  • Fixed sleep schedule
  • Reduce screen time before bed
  • Calm nighttime routine
  • Dark, quiet sleep environment

Skin & External Health

Skin reflects internal health (hydration, nutrition, sleep quality, stress levels).

  • Gentle cleansing routine
  • Moisturizing
  • Sun protection
  • Balanced diet

Mental Health & Emotional Wellbeing

Your mind is part of the system. Calm the mind to heal the body.

  • Daily mindfulness or deep breathing practices
  • Setting healthy boundaries with work and screens
  • Journaling or reflection for emotional processing
  • Connecting with nature to lower cortisol

7-Day Healthy Routine Plan

A simple daily structure to build lifelong habits.

Day 1

Hydration Focus

Start the morning with 16oz of water before anything else. Maintain consistent intake all day.

Day 2

Movement Kickstart

Incorporate a 20-minute brisk walk or light stretching routine into your day.

Day 3

Fiber & Gut Check

Add a significant fiber source (oats, leafy greens) to every meal to support digestion.

Day 4

Digital Sundown

Turn off all screens 1 hour before bed to support natural melatonin production.

Day 5

Strength & Bodyweight

Complete a 15-minute basic bodyweight circuit (squats, pushups, planks).

Day 6

Mental Decompression

Spend 15 minutes journaling, meditating, or simply sitting in silence without inputs.

Day 7

Active Recovery

Take it easy. Prioritize long walks, deep stretching, and prepping healthy meals for the next week.
